* **First‑principles over incremental tweaks** – He reduces the battery problem to electron path length and tab‑induced production stops, then rebuilds the cell from the ground up.  
* **Cost‑driven elegance** – “*simplest manufacturing process*” and “*lowest cost form factor*” show that elegance is judged by cost and manufacturability, not aesthetic.  
* **Scale‑as‑problem‑solver** – Larger cells are not a luxury; they’re a lever to achieve higher energy density *if* the thermal and mechanical issues are solved.  
* **Confidence in engineering depth** – He repeatedly stresses “*tremendous effort within Tesla engineering*,” indicating a belief that internal talent can solve problems that outsiders deem impossible.  
* **Strategic opacity** – The admission of “*not telling everything*” signals a calculated balance between public hype and protecting competitive advantage.  
* **Optimism about “hidden” breakthroughs** – The phrase “*massive breakthrough*” for something that “*nobody could figure out*” reflects his tendency to view unsolved problems as opportunities for a disruptive win.
