### 4. HOW HE REASONED  
- **Historical analogy** – compares the transition from horses to gasoline cars to the upcoming shift from fossil‑fuel to electric/solar: “*yeah i think it'd be sort of like it was a time of transition where there were horses and gasoline cars on the road at the same time*.”  
- **Physics‑first principles** – repeatedly cites “*from a first‑principle standpoint*” and “*are you conserving momentum and energy?*” to test ideas.  
- **Numbers & scaling** – uses concrete figures: “*500 million years*” for solar lifetime, “*20 000 horses*” dying per year in 19th‑century NYC, “*90 kWh*” battery packs, “*100‑fold cost reduction*” for reusable rockets.  
- **Personal anecdote as proof** – “*I thought I was insane because my mind was constantly exploding with ideas*,” showing self‑validation of his own “never‑ending explosion” of concepts.  
- **Counter‑pushback handling** – acknowledges naysayers (“*the naysayers did get me down*”) but doubles down with persistence (“*never give up unless you’re forced*”).  
- **Iterative prototyping** – stresses “*prototype as soon as possible with the least amount of money*” (Roadster hack) to prove feasibility before scaling.  
- **Cultural engineering** – borrows Google’s “20 % time” model, adapts it to SpaceX/Tesla to keep “chaos” alive.

### 5. WHAT THIS REVEALS ABOUT HOW HE THINKS  
- **Value‑driven optimism** – he frames the future as a *story* we can look forward to (“*make the future exciting*”).  
- **Existential urgency** – the “30‑year” horizon is a *deadline* for solving energy, not a vague long‑term goal.  
- **Engineer’s core** – “*I’m really primarily an engineer*” – everything is filtered through feasibility, physics, and rapid iteration.  
- **Human‑centric altruism** – despite his “super‑hero” self‑image, he repeatedly stresses love, usefulness, and making things people *love*.  
- **Risk‑tolerant but disciplined** – embraces “*eating glass and staring into the abyss of death*” as a metaphor for entrepreneurship, yet insists on data‑driven bets (e.g., solar‑share predictions).  
- **Cultural contrarian** – he enjoys “*weird things*,” from sensory‑deprivation tanks to “werewolf” jokes, indicating a willingness to explore fringe ideas if they might unlock insight.
