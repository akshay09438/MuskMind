### 4. HOW HE REASONED  

- **Analogy → Physics:** *“car as a laptop on wheels”* – reduces a complex vehicle to a familiar computing platform, highlighting the dominance of software.  
- **Scaling Numbers:** Uses **order‑of‑magnitude ratios** (10‑100×, 1 % vs 10 %) to illustrate the *non‑linear* difficulty jump from prototype to mass production.  
- **Historical Lens:** Frames Tesla’s impact as a *historical metric* (“how many years did we accelerate sustainable energy”), echoing his habit of measuring success against long‑term civilization timelines.  
- **Incremental Improvement Loop:** Each new Gigafactory is both a *production site* and a *R&D platform* (“improve the manufacturing technology”), showing his belief in **feedback‑driven engineering**.  
- **Push‑back Handling:** When confronted with the “hump” problem, he **reframes** it as a *software problem* (“factory software is extremely important”), turning a perceived limitation into an opportunity for his core competency.
