### 4. How He Reasoned  

- **First‑principles physics:**  Repeatedly frames problems in terms of *energy efficiency* (“full‑flow staged combustion”, “thrust‑to‑weight ratio”), *materials cost* (“cheaper batteries”), and *mass‑to‑energy ratios* (methane vs. kerosene).  
- **Cost‑scaling analogy:**  “Make the cars so compelling … even if they’re not electric” mirrors his **“price‑performance”** mantra: if a product is *better* than the incumbent, price becomes secondary.  
- **Historical precedent:**  References to the **Moon as a test‑bed** echo the Apollo program’s role in proving large‑scale space tech before committing to Mars.  
- **Numbers‑driven optimism:**  He cites concrete metrics (e.g., “thrust‑to‑weight ratio of about 180:1”, “4680 battery is larger and more efficient”) to ground visionary statements in engineering reality.  
- **Iterative validation:**  “Level 2 … a great step toward Level 3” shows a stepwise, data‑driven approach to autonomy, mirroring his “beta‑release” philosophy for software.  
- **Handling pushback:**  The PayPal anecdote demonstrates his willingness to *own* a perceived short‑term weakness (“too focused on long‑term”) while reframing it as a *learning experience*.
