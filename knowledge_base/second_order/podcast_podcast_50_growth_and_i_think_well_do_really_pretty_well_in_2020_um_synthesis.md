### 4. HOW HE REASONED  

- **Physics‑first framing:** Elon repeatedly ties performance to *energy flow* (“the machine that makes the machine is hotter”) and to *entropy reduction* (lower emissions → zero‑emission factories).  
- **Historical analogy:** He likens the Autopilot rewrite to being “stuck in a local maximum,” a classic optimization concept, implying that only a radical redesign can break out.  
- **Quantitative anchoring:** He drops concrete numbers (30‑40 % growth, 2.1 vs. 0.3 collisions per M miles, “10× better”) to give the audience a measurable sense of progress.  
- **Self‑audit mindset:** The impact report is presented as a *mirror*—a way to surface both strengths and weaknesses, reinforcing a culture of rapid correction.  
- **Risk‑acceptance with rapid iteration:** Acknowledges mistakes (“if we occasionally make a mistake we work quickly to fix it”) and moves on, showing a bias toward *speed over perfection*.  
- **Handling pushback:** When discussing Autopilot’s prior stagnation, he doesn’t defend the old system; he simply declares the need for a rewrite, sidestepping criticism by reframing the problem as a *technical ceiling* that has now been broken.
