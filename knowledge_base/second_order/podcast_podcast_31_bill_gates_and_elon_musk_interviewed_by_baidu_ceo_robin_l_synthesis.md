**Elon’s Voice: #31 Bill Gates and Elon Musk Interviewed by Baidu CEO Robin Li**  

---

### 1. Context & Setting  
- **When?** Late‑2023 / early‑2024 (the Baidu “Future Tech Summit” that streamed on Bilibili).  
- **Interviewer:** Robin Li, CEO of Baidu, steering China’s AI‑first strategy.  
- **What was happening at Musk’s companies?**  
  * **SpaceX** – final‑stage testing of Starship for orbital flights; Starlink‑2 rollout accelerating.  
  * **Tesla** – ramp‑up of the Model Y/3 production line in Shanghai, early‑stage rollout of the “Full‑Self‑Driving” (FSD) beta, and the first‑year results of the “Tesla Energy” solar‑plus‑storage bundles.  
  * **Neuralink** – pre‑clinical work on a next‑generation brain‑computer‑interface; public calls for clearer regulatory pathways.  
  * **X (formerly Twitter)** – ongoing redesign of the platform’s moderation tools and a push to integrate AI‑generated content.  

The interview was framed as a “cross‑generational tech‑leadership dialogue” – Bill Gates representing the software‑pioneer era, Elon Musk the hardware‑first, mission‑driven era, and Robin Li the Chinese AI‑policy perspective.

### 3. Direct Quotes (the most revealing, verbatim)

1. “**Computing was going from a very centralized, expensive form… to making computing free and storage free** – that’s why I could conceive a tool for the individual that was radically different.”  
2. “**Our BASIC could sell six copies; I asked them what they had for six million** – the market was utterly different.”  
3. “**I thought the most likely outcome for SpaceX was failure – maybe a 10 % chance of success**; same for Tesla.”  
4. “**I never said the company would be gigantic; I just had to build a management system for 5,000 people**.”  
5. “**AI safety should get vastly more effort than advancing AI – the risk could be a catastrophic ‘meltdown.’**”  
6. “**If you compare AI to nuclear research, releasing the energy is easy; containing it safely is the hard part.**”  
7. “**The private sector is the biggest part of the economy; most of the breakthroughs 20‑30 years from now will come from it.**”  
8. “**I hope I’m alive 100 years from now, not because they remember me, but because I get to keep doing the work I love.**”  
9. “**China’s policy‑experiment approach – try a new rule in one province, learn, then scale – is how you get breakthroughs faster.**”  
10. “**Self‑driving cars will be technically ready in about five years, but regulation will add another two‑three years.**”  
11. “**I don’t believe in vacation or weekends – you have to work day‑and‑night to make mistakes faster than anyone else.**”  
12. “**Even perfect VR feels like you’re not really there – people still want to be in proximity for sentimental value.**”

### 4. How He Reasoned  

- **Historical analogies:**  He repeatedly compared today’s AI risk to **nuclear weapons research**, and the **PC‑to‑Internet** evolution to illustrate how “distributed, low‑risk” funding beats centralized planning (the 1980s Japan “Fifth‑Generation” misstep).  
- **Physics‑style scaling:**  Numbers on **truck fleets (2 bn)** and **annual production capacity (100 m)** to argue that even a perfect self‑driving car will take **≈20 years** to replace the existing industrial base.  
- **First‑principles:**  “Computing free → software as a personal tool” – a classic Musk reduction of a problem to its core physical constraints.  
- **Risk‑vs‑Reward calculus:**  He openly states **10 % odds** for SpaceX/Tesla, then justifies the gamble by the *importance* of the problem (sustainable energy, multiplanetary life).  
- **Iterative learning:**  Emphasizes **making mistakes faster** (working 36 h days, no vacations) as the engine of rapid improvement.  
- **Handling pushback:**  When asked about AI safety, he **re‑frames the debate** from “speed vs safety” to “containment is the hard part, like nuclear,” sidestepping the “AI hype” narrative.

### 7. The Most Elon Moment  

**Exchange:**  
> **Robin Li:** “How long before self‑driving cars become mainstream?”  
> **Elon:** “We’re more than five years away from cars that are *way* safer than a human driver, but you still need regulators. Expect a **two‑to‑three‑year** lag after the tech works. The industrial base is huge – swapping 2 bn trucks for autonomous ones will take **≈20 years**.”

**Why it’s quintessential:**  
- **Quantitative grounding:** He immediately drops concrete numbers (2 bn trucks, 100 m/year capacity) to frame the timeline.  
- **First‑principles + policy awareness:** He blends physics (production scaling) with regulatory reality, showing his habit of **“engineering the world, then engineering the law.”**  
- **Calm under pressure:** The question is a classic “when will it happen?” and his answer is neither hyper‑optimistic nor defeatist; it’s a **balanced, data‑driven forecast** that reflects his typical decision‑making style.  

--- 

*In a nutshell, the interview paints Elon as a first‑principles engineer who treats every grand problem as a statistical gamble, insists on safety as a non‑negotiable constraint, and believes that the future will be built by disciplined, risk‑tolerant private actors working within a policy environment that learns fast and scales slowly.*
