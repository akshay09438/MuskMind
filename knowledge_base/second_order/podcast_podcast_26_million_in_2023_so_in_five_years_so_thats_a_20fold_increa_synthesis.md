### 4. HOW HE REASONED  

- **Numbers → Intuition:** Elon repeatedly converts raw counts (26 M kWh, 5 000 cars/week) into *percent* changes to illustrate the *scale* of exponential growth.  
- **Analogy to Area‑Under‑Curve:** He likens 2017’s production “area under the curve” to a tiny slice of an exponential ramp, explaining why the media saw a “shock.”  
- **Physics‑style “order‑of‑magnitude” thinking:** He treats a six‑month shift as a *small* delta in a *large* exponential timeline, akin to a phase‑lag in a wave.  
- **Historical correction:** He references the 2017 criticism as a case study, using it to teach that “linear thinkers” misinterpret early‑stage exponential data.  
- **Handling pushback:** He acknowledges criticism (“people didn’t understand”) but reframes it as a *knowledge gap* rather than a flaw in the forecast.  
- **Future‑oriented framing:** By saying “these are for the future,” he positions his numbers as *directional guides* rather than hard promises, pre‑empting accusations of over‑promising.

### 5. WHAT THIS REVEALS ABOUT HOW HE THINKS  

- **Exponential Mindset:** Elon’s default lens is *exponential*; any linear framing is, to him, a cognitive error.  
- **Data‑First, Narrative‑Second:** He leans on raw metrics (kWh, cars/week) and then builds a story, rather than the reverse.  
- **Tolerance for Ambiguity:** He openly calls his forecasts “guess work,” yet proceeds with high confidence—showing comfort with uncertainty as long as the *trend* is clear.  
- **Communication as Teaching:** He treats the interview as a classroom, aiming to upgrade the audience’s mental model (“we want people to understand”).  
- **Strategic Framing of Delays:** Small schedule slips are down‑played; the narrative focuses on *long‑term trajectory* rather than short‑term variance.  
- **Risk‑Averse to Perception, Not to Execution:** He worries more about *how the press frames numbers* than about the actual engineering risk.
