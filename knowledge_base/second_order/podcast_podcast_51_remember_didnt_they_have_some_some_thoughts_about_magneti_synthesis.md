### 5. WHAT THIS REVEALS ABOUT HOW HE THINKS  

* **Physics‑first mindset:** He reduces every idea to a force‑balance or energy‑budget before considering business or hype.  
* **Pragmatic optimism:** He will *pursue* a concept (vacuum tunnel, maglev) if the physics checks out, but will *immediately discard* anything that creates a safety or engineering nightmare (surface magnet road).  
* **Long‑term risk aversion:** Climate change is framed as a “dumb experiment” that must be stopped; he treats the hidden subsidy of fossil fuels as a moral debt that will be repaid.  
* **Scale‑aware:** He constantly references production bottlenecks (battery gigafactories, fleet turnover) and knows that breakthroughs must be manufacturable at billions‑of‑units scale.  
* **Iterative, test‑bed approach:** The Boring tunnels are “test versions” of a larger vision, reflecting his habit of building a minimal viable product, learning, then scaling.  
* **Skepticism of quick fixes:** “Clean coal” and “carbon capture” are dismissed because they lack physical plausibility at the required scale.  
* **Energy hierarchy:** Solar → batteries → electric propulsion → rockets (the only remaining fuel‑burning domain).
