### 7. The Most Elon Moment  

**Exchange:** When asked why SpaceX continues to pour money into reusability despite already high launch rates, Elon replies:  

> “It’s much like a 747. It costs about as much to refuel our rocket as it does to refuel a 747… we need to make full reusability work, and then we can bring the cost of an expendable launch from 5 % down to 4 % of the vehicle price.”  

**Why it’s quintessential:**  
- **Physics‑first framing** (comparing rockets to a 747).  
- **Laser‑focused cost reduction** (pinpointing the 5 %→4 % margin).  
- **Vision of “clever” engineering** (the reusability elements) that turns a marginal gain into a **paradigm shift**.  
- **A blend of humility (“just a few hundred thousand dollars”) and ambition (full reusability).**  

This snippet captures Elon’s **habit of turning a mundane number into a strategic lever**, the **relentless pursuit of engineering elegance**, and the **ability to articulate a massive market‑level impact from a single engineering insight**.
