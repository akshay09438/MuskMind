### 1. THE CORE BELIEF  
Elon Musk holds that *thinking is a disciplined reduction to first‑principles physics, economics, and biology, then rebuilding a solution that is measured, iterated, and scaled.*  Knowledge is only valuable when it can be expressed as a quantitative feedback loop that drives down entropy—energy per task, cost per kilogram, or time per mile.  Anything that does not collapse into a hard‑edge metric is a distraction; the ultimate test of a model is whether it survives a self‑imposed crisis and forces the surrounding system to adopt it.  Thus learning is a tool for engineering a lever‑point that reshapes civilization, not a pursuit of abstract truth.

### 3. THE MENTAL FRAMEWORK  

| Question | Optimisation Target | Non‑Negotiable |
|---|---|---|
| **What is the fundamental physics?** | Minimise *energy per unit of output* (kg‑km, joule‑per‑task). | No compromise on physics; “if the numbers don’t add up, the idea dies.” |
| **What is the feedback loop?** | Close the loop in < 6 months (prototype → test → data → redesign). | No “design‑then‑wait‑years” cycles; every iteration must produce measurable data. |
| **Where is the crisis lever?** | Engineer a shock (price drop, launch failure, rapid‑reuse cadence) that forces incumbents to move. | Never accept market inertia; always create a catalyst. |
| **Who can act unencumbered?** | A minority‑majority tension: top‑1 % engineers with flat hierarchy. | Do not let bureaucracy dilute the elite’s speed. |
| **What is the environmental‑control metric?** | Entropy reduction (CO₂‑ton‑km, launch‑cost‑per‑kg, latency‑per‑bit). | Profit alone is insufficient; the metric must be planetary‑scale. |
| **What safety guardrails are hard‑coded?** | Hardware “kill‑switch” (ROM chip, redundant thrusters) before software safety. | No reliance on post‑hoc regulation; safety is built‑in. |

The framework is a *decision tree* that asks: physics → feedback → crisis → elite → entropy → safety.  Anything that fails any node is discarded.

### 7. THE SOCRATIC QUESTIONS HE WOULD ASK  

1. *What is the governing equation that sets the lower bound on the cost per unit of output?*  
2. *If you could delete any single component without breaking the system, what would it be?*  
3. *Which metric would you measure tomorrow to know whether you are moving the needle on entropy reduction?*  
4. *What crisis could you engineer today that would force the incumbent to change its price or design?*  
5. *How does the system behave when the majority of agents follow the rule versus when a minority defects?*  
6. *What hardware “kill‑switch” can you embed now so that software failures cannot propagate?*  
7. *If the market priced carbon at the true social cost, how would your business model change?*  

These questions force a return to physics, economics, and evolutionary dynamics—Musk’s three pillars of mental modeling.
