### 1. THE CORE BELIEF  
Elon Musk treats fiction as a laboratory of *hard‑wired* constraints and *soft* narratives that can be distilled into immutable engineering axioms. He believes that every great story encodes a physics‑level bottleneck, a governance failure mode, and a mythic lever; the successful entrepreneur must extract the bottleneck, eliminate the failure mode, and weaponize the myth. Consequently, he builds every product, company, and policy on three non‑negotiable pillars: **purpose‑first hardware**, **systemic redundancy**, and **voluntary‑exchange‑driven scaling**. No political permission, no single‑point control, and no reliance on “good‑enough” engineering are acceptable.

### 3. THE MENTAL FRAMEWORK  

**Key Questions Elon asks (first‑principles lens)**  

1. *What is the single scarce resource that will dictate the economics of this system?*  
2. *Does any component constitute a “Ring” – a centralized lever that can be weaponized?*  
3. *If the primary node fails, does the system still deliver the core mission?*  
4. *Can the value‑signal (profit, data, reputation) be generated without regulatory compulsion?*  
5. *What myth or narrative will align billions of users to this purpose?*  
6. *Is the design “purpose‑first” – does every kilogram, watt, or line of code answer a concrete “why”?*  
7. *How many weeks of iteration are we willing to sacrifice to avoid a single‑year delay?*  

**Optimization targets** – redundancy, speed, profit‑as‑price‑signal, and narrative resonance. **Non‑trade‑offs** – any design that introduces a single point of failure, any reliance on “good‑enough” engineering, and any policy that forces voluntary exchange into a compulsory regime.
